# DORA Regulation — ICT Risk and Third-Party AI

## Article 28 — General principles of ICT third-party risk management
Financial entities must identify, assess, and manage ICT third-party risk. AI-generated code changes to production systems constitute a third-party ICT service under DORA. Any material change to a critical or important function must be subject to pre-deployment risk assessment and documented approval.

## Article 30 — Key contractual provisions
Before deploying AI-modified code to production, financial entities must ensure: (1) the change has been independently verified, (2) the risk has been assessed and documented, (3) a named accountable officer has approved the change. Failure to document CRO sign-off on high-blast-radius changes constitutes a DORA compliance breach.

## Material Change Definition
A change is material if it affects: data integrity, calculation logic used in financial reporting, file layouts used by downstream systems, or any function processing more than £1M/day in transactions.

## Enforcement
From January 2025, EU/UK regulators may fine institutions up to 2% of global annual turnover for undocumented material ICT changes. Signed audit certificates are the primary evidence regulators request in ICT incident investigations.
